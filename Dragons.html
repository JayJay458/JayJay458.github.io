JAPANESE DRAGONS

How does nature do it?

Rare photo of a baby dragon snapped in the wild
credit Toonami

Unlike the traditional Chinese and European dragons, which begin life as a smaller version of their serpeantine and quadripedal forms, the Japanese dragon is unique in that it undergoes rather severe physical changes as it progresses through the beautiful stages of life.



INFANCY



The Japanese dragon begins life as a rambucious ball, akin to that pink slime they sell severely overpriced at carnivals and malls, that you beg your parents to buy for you, except these have teeth.
As they lack arms, legs, necks, and torsos, they are pretty useless, so nature has compensated by making them too cute to kill.
They can typically be found clinging to human children and consume nothing except protein and "human friendship" and "heart of the cards" or whatever.











ADOLESCENCE



As the Japanese dragon ages, it go through the typical amphibian process of growing arms, legs, and a really stubby tail; except instead of growing large lungs, it gains the ability to breathe fireballs. This stage is also pretty useless, but at least it almost looks cool











ADULT



Once the Japanese dragon has gained enough friendship points, it follows the natural evolutionary trail for reptiles and sprouts a full suit of medieval fantasy armor, complete with massively oversized talons and the ability to fly at hypersonic speeds.
Where does it come from? How does it fit? Can they take it off to use the bathroom? The world may never know.











FULL MATURITY



Following the train to its logical conclusion, the humble Japanese dragon will eventually blossom into the beautiful American-made AC130 Gunship, complete with 25mm, 40mm, and even 105mm cannons mounted on either side, raining glorious freedom onto the heads, and schools, of anyone within half a hemester that doesn't have enough freedom in their hearts.
Truly, nature is a beautiful thing.
